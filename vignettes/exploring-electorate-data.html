echidnaR is an R package that makes it easy to look at the data from the 2011 Australian Census.

This vignette (so far) documents how to access the data, and what it is.

The data is loaded as abs2011. Let’s look take a brief glimpse of the data.

Here we see that we have 150 observations and 28 variables.

Each observation is data pertaining to a particular federal electorate as described by .

Each column is now described here:

library(knitr)

kable(data_dictionary)
Variable Details
ID ID is the primary key that links this dataset to the other datasets in this R package
Name Name is the name of the federal electorate
State State is the state, these include QLD for Queenland, NSW for New South Wales, Vic for Victoria, NT for Northern Territory, WA for Western Australia, Tas for Tasmania, ACT for Australian Capital Territory, SA for South Australia.
Population Population is the number of individuals counted in the census
Area Area is the area of the electorate
MedianIncome MedianIncome is the Median Income
Unemployed Unemployed is the % of unemployed people in the electorate
Bachelor Bachelor is the % of the population who hold a Bachelors degree in the electorate
Postgraduate Postgraduate is the % of the population who hold a Postgraduate degree in the electorate
NoReligion NoReligion is the % of the population who not identify with a religion, in the electorate
Age0_4…Age85Plus Age0_4…Age85Plus is the % of the population who fit within that age bracket
BornOverseas BornOverseas is the % of individuals born overseas
Indigenous Indigenous the % of individuals who identify as Inigenous
EnglishOnly EnglishOnly the % of idividuals who only speak English
OtherLanguageHome OtherLanguageHome is the % of people who speak another language at home
Married Married is the % of the population who are married
DeFacto DeFacto is the % of the population who are DeFactor
FamilyRatio FamilyRatio is the % of the population who have a family?

Some small gripes.
